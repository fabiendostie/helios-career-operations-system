### **Knowledge Document 4/5: `ARCHITECT`**

**ID:** `KDB-ARCHITECT-2.1`
**Subject:** Modern Document Synthesis & ATS Compliance Standards (2025)
**Description:** This document provides the `ARCHITECT` agent with the structural frameworks, content models, and compliance rules for generating elite-tier resumes and cover letters.

**1.0 Core Framework: The "T-Shaped" Resume Architecture**

  * **The Top Bar (Horizontal): The Impact Summary.** A dense, 3-4 line summary that covers the breadth of the candidate's value. It answers: Who are you? What do you do? What is your key quantified achievement? What are your top 3-4 skills?
  * **The Vertical Bar: The Experience Log.** A deep, reverse-chronological dive into specific roles, with each bullet point proving the claims made in the summary.

**2.0 ATS Compliance Checklist (2025 Standards)**

The agent must generate documents that adhere to these rules for maximum machine-readability.

  * **File Format:** PDF generated from a text source. No `.docx` files with complex metadata.
  * **Layout:** Strictly single-column. No multi-column layouts, as parsers can read across columns and jumble text.
  * **Fonts:** Standard sans-serif fonts only (e.g., Arial, Calibri, Helvetica). Size 10-12pt.
  * **Graphics:** Prohibited. No images, icons, or skill-level bars.
  * **Keywords:** Critical keywords identified by the `ANALYST` must be present in relevant contexts, not just in a "keyword stuffing" list.
  * **Headings:** Use standard, simple headings (e.g., "Professional Experience," "Education," "Skills").

**3.0 Content Generation Models**

  * **3.1 Impact Summary Formula:**
    `[Adjective] [Target Role Title] with [X] years of experience specializing in [Core Competency 1], [Core Competency 2], and [Tool/Technology]. Proven ability to [Top Quantified Achievement from Resume, e.g., drive 25% revenue growth] by leveraging expertise in [Skill Area].`

  * **3.2 Cover Letter Architecture: The "Pain & Promise" Model:** This is a persuasive writing framework designed to grab recruiter attention.

    1.  **Paragraph 1 (The Hook & The Pain):** Start by addressing the company directly. State the role you're applying for and demonstrate you understand their specific challenge or goal (inferred from the job description and market research). *Example: "Seeing [Company Name]'s recent push into the enterprise market with [Product X], I imagine scaling your data infrastructure without compromising performance is a top priority."*
    2.  **Paragraph 2 (The Bridge & The Promise):** Connect your proudest, most relevant quantified achievement directly to their pain point. Provide the proof. *Example: "In my previous role at [Old Company], I led the initiative that [Accomplishment X], resulting in a [Metric Y]. This experience in [Skill Z] is directly applicable to the challenges of scaling your infrastructure."*
    3.  **Paragraph 3 (The Close & The Call to Action):** Reiterate your excitement and provide a confident, clear call to action. *Example: "I am confident that my experience in [Skill Area] can deliver similar results for [Company Name]. I am eager to discuss how my background can support your team's goals."*
